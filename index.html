Your Role: 3 Simple Tips for Accessible Resources

You don't need to be an AT expert to make a huge difference. Building accessibility into your resources from the start is the most effective way to support all students.

1

Use True Headings

Instead of just making text bold and large, use the built-in "Heading 1," "Heading 2" styles in Word and your LMS. This creates a digital map that screen readers use for navigation.

2

Add "Alt Text" to Images

Right-click any image and select "Edit Alt Text." Write a short description of the image's *meaning* or *purpose*. This is essential for students who are blind or have low vision.

3

Run the Accessibility Checker

Before publishing, use the built-in accessibility checker in Microsoft Office and other tools. It's like a spell-checker for inclusion and will find most common errors for you.
